# Brief: training

## Facts

- Two offerings, separate sign-ups, most athletes do both, either alone is fine. Both free,
  donations optional and need-blind.
- Summer training: June 1 to August 19, 2026. Monday/Wednesday/Friday. Practice starts 10:30 AM
  sharp; late arrivals miss the group.
  - Mon 10:30–12:15, East High, no carpool: strength, core, spenst, some running.
  - Wed 10:30–12:15: hill intervals and bounding.
  - Fri 10:30–12:30: over-distance mountain running.
  - Off days (Tue/Thu/weekends): independent workouts posted in CrewLAB, biking and roller-ski
    options, can be done solo or with teammates.
  - Locations rotate: East High, Hillside (Hilltop parking lot), Kincaid Park, Bartlett trails,
    Chugach front range trailheads. Spot posted in CrewLAB, can change.
  - Carpool for off-site days: leaves East High, arrive by 10:00 AM; return carpool back to East;
    parents may pick up at East or at the site.
- Activities: running (trail, road, mountain) and strength/core (bounding, plyometrics, spenst,
  mostly outdoors, no gym) at group practice; biking (singletrack, gravel) and roller-skiing on
  independent days.
- Helmets: required for every roller-ski and mountain-bike session, no exceptions.
- Training groups: one team, three groups by experience and pace. Foundation (new to organized
  training), Devos (development: younger/newer with some background), Comp (experienced, aiming
  at state meets and the Besh Cup series). Workouts and weekly load set per group to manage
  overtraining and overuse risk.
- Eligibility: rising 9th through 12th, plus graduates home from college (encouraged into
  volunteer leadership). No junior high. No ski experience needed.
- Gear to bring: water, snacks, trail shoes, layers (weather changes fast), helmet on wheel days.
  Loaner roller skis, poles, and more available; ask a coach.
- First session: waiver on file first; Monday = East High by 10:30; off-site day = East by 10:00
  for carpool or check CrewLAB; no roller skis or bike needed for a first run.
- Talkeetna camp: July 21 (morning departure) to July 24 (evening return), 2026. Four days, three
  nights, cabins on a lake near Talkeetna, dry cabin (outhouses, no showers). Amy Purevsuren runs
  it. Open to athletes who do not attend summer training. Training twice a day: running,
  roller-skiing, strength, hill bounding with poles. Between sessions: lake (boats, paddleboards,
  swimming) and a sauna; evenings group meals. Free, donations by what a family can manage. Camp
  registration and consent are separate forms, in CrewLAB. Exact site and address go to
  registered families in CrewLAB.
- Camp packing list: sleeping bag/pillow, training clothes cool+warm, rain gear, hangout clothes,
  socks/personal clothing, swimsuit+towel, running shoes, bounding poles, roller-ski kit if you
  roller-ski (boots, skis, poles, helmet), water bottle with carrier, hygiene items, daily meds,
  optional personal food for dietary needs, optional snacks.
- Glossary terms: spenst (explosive jumping work), over-distance/OD (long easy conversational
  pace), Besh Cup (Alaska's six-race XC ski series over three winter weekends; selects Team
  Alaska for Junior Nationals, and AWG in even years).
