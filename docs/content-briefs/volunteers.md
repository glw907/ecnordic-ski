# Brief: volunteers

## Facts

- ECXC runs entirely on unpaid volunteers: parents, former racers, local athletes.
- Geoff Wright: leads the summer session. Lifelong runner and cyclist; came to XC skiing as an
  adult; 13 years coaching NSAA's Junior Nordic program, eight as site director at Kincaid;
  assistant coach at West High 2024/25; now coaches at Bettye Davis East; raised three
  competitive runner-skiers, youngest now with the APU Nordic Elite Team. Personal: snow, trails,
  books, general nerdery, Sunday morning pancakes.
- Amy Purevsuren: runs the Talkeetna camp; head nordic coach at Bettye Davis East High School.
  Raced for Western Colorado: seven-time All-American, 1997 national title in the 15K freestyle,
  first woman in school history with an individual skiing national championship. Teaches high
  school English; started in the Peace Corps in Mongolia; 11 years teaching in rural Alaska,
  focused on making students lifelong readers and writers. Lives in Anchorage with her husband
  and three kids; the family reads, camps, gets outside.
- Duncan Wright (Geoff's oldest): coaches Friday distance sessions. Competitive mountain runner,
  cyclist, skier; top-10 at Crow Pass and the Kesugi Ridge 50K (youngest in the field); second
  place at the Kenai 250, a 250-mile mountain bike race. Outdoor Studies degree from APU,
  snow science concentration. Personal: science fiction, trail work, building bikes, woodworking.
- Glossary: NSAA (Nordic Skiing Association of Anchorage: grooms the city's ski trails, runs
  community programs including Junior Nordic). APU (Alaska Pacific University; APU Nordic is one
  of the country's top XC ski clubs).
- Help wanted, the three most useful things: drive (remote trailheads, carpool; a driver with
  room for a few athletes makes a session happen); train alongside athletes at any pace (the
  endurance-day spread is wide; adults wanted with faster and slower kids); teach what you know
  (strength, technique, nutrition, navigation).
- Graduates home from college: especially welcome to train and step into team leadership.
- No coaching certification or ski background required to help.
