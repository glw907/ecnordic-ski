# Coach Voice Corpus

Real passages from working endurance-sport programs that coach high school athletes and their
parents, collected to ground the ECXC voice in examples rather than rules alone. The
`content-guide.md` exemplar pairs and the calibration gold set in the shared method are
self-authored; this corpus is the outside check, showing how good coaches actually write to teens
and families in running, nordic skiing, and rowing.

## How to use it

Before drafting a page or post, skim the lessons index, then read the two or three entries closest
to what you are writing. Imitate the stance and the concreteness, never the wording. The point is to
absorb how a coach sounds when they trust the reader: plain sentences, real details, honesty about
commitment and cost, welcome without a sales pitch.

Three of the entries (APU, Chugiak-Eagle River, Juneau) are Alaska nordic programs in ECXC's own
backyard. They are the best context match and the most dangerous to copy, since a neighboring
program's phrasing lifted onto ecxc.ski would be obvious. Study the stance; write your own words.

## Provenance and honesty

Each excerpt is quoted from the live site at the URL given, retrieved 2026-06-09, and every entry
was spot-verified verbatim against the raw page text the same day. Source typos are preserved
deliberately ("if its Grandpa's birthday", "rowing is water sport", "we are all RUN the same
course"); do not correct them, since they are part of the verbatim record and arguably part of the
charm. These are short excerpts held for internal voice study, not content to republish. Quoted
passages sit in fenced blocks because they carry punctuation and vocabulary the prose guard blocks
in our own writing; the annotation under each is the payload. The corpus grows over time as Geoff
adds his own edited ECXC content (see First-party gold at the end).

## Lessons index

The recurring qualities these exemplars teach, mapped to the guide's dimensions:

- **Welcome without a sales pitch.** State that no experience is needed as a plain fact, not an
  exclamation. (Run Waconia, Juneau, Norwalk, CRLS)
- **Honesty about commitment.** Name what the sport asks before the reader signs up. (Pioneer Crew,
  CRLS, Run Waconia)
- **Trust the athlete's agency.** The effort is theirs; the coach can invite, not force.
  (Clarkston, Coach Saltmarsh)
- **Concrete and local.** Real places, real schedules, the actual shape of a session. (APU,
  Chugiak-Eagle River)
- **Plain reassurance for parents.** Answer the real worry (safety, cost, swimming) matter-of-factly,
  without a guarantee. (Norwalk, CRLS, Juneau)
- **Fun named as fun.** For younger and newer athletes, the draw is enjoyment, said outright.
  (Chugiak-Eagle River, Juneau, Coach Saltmarsh)

## Running

### Clarkston Cross Country, guide for prospective runners and parents

Sport: high school cross country. https://clarkstoncrosscountry.org/guide-for-prospective-runners-and-parents

```
"The spark must come from the athlete. The coach can encourage but not motivate."

"If homework is heavy, if its Grandpa's birthday, if their stomach is not quite right or if
there is any pain from running at all, your son or daughter should feel comfortable taking time
away from the sport."
```

Trusts the athlete's agency and puts real life above rigid rules. The second passage answers a
parent's worry by naming ordinary situations (homework, a birthday, an off stomach) instead of
stating a policy. Concrete cases read as permission; an abstract rule reads as fine print.

### Run Waconia, 7-12 XC Basics

Sport: youth and high school cross country. https://www.runwaconia.com/xc-basics

```
"Cross Country is a unique sport in which everyone is on the starting team, no one sits on the
bench, and we are all RUN the same course trying to get to the finish line a bit faster than the
last time we tried."

"Daily workouts will be varied throughout the week and all students are given a choice as to how
hard they want to work daily! (you get what you put in)"
```

The welcome is structural, not stated. No bench, no cuts, everyone races. The effort-outcome link
lands in a four-word parenthetical rather than a paragraph of motivation.

## Nordic skiing (local Alaska)

### APU Nordic Ski Center, Junior Program

Sport: high school nordic skiing, Anchorage. https://apunordic.com/programs/junior/

```
"Training is typically located at Hillside, APU, and Kincaid. Athletes will also take special
training sessions to run in the mountains or ski other trails."

"Sessions are 3-6 times per week. Activities include skate and classic skiing, running, ski
imitation, classic and skate roller skiing, and strength."
```

A model of local concreteness: named trailheads, a real session count, the actual list of training
modes, no adjectives. This is the same Anchorage terrain ECXC trains on, so it shows the bar for
specificity. Match the precision; name ECXC's own places and schedule.

### Chugiak-Eagle River Nordic Ski Club, Junior Nordic

Sport: youth nordic skiing, Eagle River. https://www.cernsc.org/junior-nordic/

```
"We subscribe to the Norwegian system of teaching the love of skiing through fun on skis rather
than skills and drills, particularly at the younger levels."

"A typical 6-year old session might include a game of wolves and caribou, a short ski, the limbo,
another short ski, some red-light green-light and maybe some ski time playing on the big hill."
```

Names fun as the method and then proves it with a concrete play-by-play. The session description
does the persuading; no sentence has to claim the program is fun, because the reader can see it.

### Juneau Nordic Ski Club, Youth Programs

Sport: youth nordic skiing, Juneau. https://jnski.org/youth-programs/

```
"No previous ski experience is required. All we ask that each student bring a commitment the
entire season, a desire to try new things, improve your skiing skills, and a willingness to
support others doing the same!"

"No one is denied participation in our programs based on ability to pay."
```

Pairs an open door with a clear ask in the same breath: no experience needed, but show up for the
season and support the others. The cost line is one flat sentence, no euphemism, which is exactly
how ECXC states that training is free.

## Rowing

### Pioneer Crew, Novice Parent Info

Sport: youth rowing. https://pioneercrew.org/novice/

```
"Practice is mandatory. Period. The reason why, other than that we're teaching your child to be
responsible for their commitments, is that the boat can't go out without all eight rowers and a
coxswain."

"We do everything in our power to stay on top of the water, not in it."
```

Sets a hard expectation and then gives the real reason, which happens to be unanswerable: the boat
physically cannot launch a rower short. The safety line is memorable because it is plain and a
little dry, not because it promises anything.

### Norwalk River Rowing, youth FAQ

Sport: youth rowing. https://norwalkriverrowing.org/youth/frequently-asked-questions/

```
"Yes, rowing is water sport. There is often a little splash or spray while rowing. However, it is
unlikely that your boat will capsize."

"The great thing about rowing is that the entire boat crosses the finish line together no matter
what size the rowers are. You can be any size and still row well."
```

Answers the unspoken fear (we will tip over) by naming the small true thing first, then the
reassurance, without overpromising. The body-type answer removes a real barrier with a specific
fact about how the sport works.

### CRLS Rowing, FAQ

Sport: high school rowing. https://crlsrowing.org/about/faq/

```
"Practices are 5-6 days a week. Only high winds, lightning and thunder keep us off the water. On
those days, we do workouts on land."

"Sure. But do so with the understanding that if you miss practice, you'll have missed some things
and that will make it harder for a coach to put you into a boat when you come back."
```

States the commitment as a fact and names the exact weather that stops practice, so a parent knows
attentive people run the program. The second answer allows flexibility and names the trade-off in
the same sentence. No sugar-coating, no lecture.

## Instructive contrast: even real coaches drift

A high school coach writing about team culture (Coach Saltmarsh,
https://coachsaltmarsh.com/high-school-cross-country-track-team-culture-and-success/) lands his
strongest lines when he is concrete and his weakest when he reaches for consultant language. Both
are from the same author, which is the lesson.

On-voice, concrete and self-aware:

```
"And, if your team is anything like mine, you know that half the reason they joined the team was
to be invited to the team dinners."

"stop coaching runners like robots and connect with them as individuals"
```

The same writer, drifting into the catalog (note "fosters," "infuses an element," the flat
truism):

```
"It not only fosters a sense of unity but also infuses an element of excitement and anticipation
to the day."

"A tight-knit team culture can significantly impact a team's performance."
```

The on-voice lines name a specific, slightly funny truth (kids come for the dinners). The off-voice
lines could appear on any program's page for any sport. When an ECXC draft starts sounding like the
second pair, cut back to the first.

## First-party gold

Geoff's own edited passages, the highest-authority exemplars in this corpus, since they are the
actual target voice rather than a neighbor's. Empty until the first harvest; the baseline above
stands in.

### The harvest procedure

Run on request ("feed my edits back"), after Geoff ships an edit to a page or post:

1. Diff the shipped file against the draft it replaced (`git log --follow` the file; the draft is
   the last commit authored through `content-draft`).
2. Extract the before/after sentence pairs where he rewrote rather than trimmed. Each pair is a
   ready-made off-voice/on-voice exemplar in the method's own format.
3. Add the strongest passages here as gold entries: the source page, the passage, the one-line
   lesson. Two or three per harvest; this section stays curated, not exhaustive.
4. Promote any fix he has now made twice into a load-bearing rule or recipe note in
   `docs/content-guide.md`, naming the pattern it kills.
5. Where a gold entry teaches the same lesson as a third-party entry above, the gold entry wins;
   mark the third-party entry as superseded rather than deleting it.
