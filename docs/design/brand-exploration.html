Logo & Color Exploration

Two decisions on one page. A more professional cut of the EC-over-XC mark that holds up on a ballcap or tee, and a color identity that no other Anchorage program owns.

Decided 2026-06-11 (final, after three rejected cuts): the mark is the tile system: Nunito Black letters (the site's own display face) knocked out of four rounded square tiles. This is the classic solved pattern for four-letters-in-a-square (Scrabble tiles, app-icon badges, heraldic quartering): square containers carry the grid, so the letters keep their natural proportions. Three context cuts from one generator (build-mark.py): the four-tile primary (nav, large uses), a compact single-badge cut (favicon), and the crest (apparel). Rejected on the way: stretched font glyphs (every square-module candidate needed 7–25% per-letter stretch; visible threshold ~3%), sharp bespoke geometry (read as a computer font against the humanist voice), and rounded bespoke geometry (the warmth was invisible at nav size). Palette B won, retuned after review: fireweed is a true roadside pink, oklch(66% 0.21 357), and spruce is Alaskan black spruce, oklch(25% 0.035 175). Because fireweed is lighter than spruce, usage inverts: spruce carries the weight (the site's header and footer bands), fireweed is the flash on top, like a pink mark on a dark spruce hoodie. Links and buttons on white use a deeper fireweed cut, oklch(52% 0.21 357), to clear text contrast. Site fonts are unchanged; the logo faces live only as converted outlines.

01 · The colors we can't use

Verified before designing. East High is red and blue (Wikipedia). APU Nordic wears the university's blue with white. Winter Stars is red. UAA is green and gold, which constrains how a green direction gets tuned. The current site is crimson plus cobalt, which sits inside all three named zones, so this is a repaint rather than a nudge.

02 · Three cuts of EC over XC

The lockup stays as conceived: EC over XC, the two C's stacked on the right. What changes is the letterforms. All three concepts are shown in neutral ink; any palette below can drive them. Each includes a favicon-size test and a one-color inverse, since embroidery is a one-or-two-color medium.

Concept 1 · Varsity Stack

Alumni Sans Black. A typeface drawn from American varsity and athletic-department lettering, which is exactly the register a school-community program wants.

The bare wordmark, no container. Both rows justify to the same width so the C's form a clean right rail and the negative space between rows does the work the old grid gutters did. This is the closest evolution of the current mark: same idea, professional letterforms.

On a cap it embroiders as four bold letters, one thread color, no fine detail to fuzz. On the site it sits light in the header, like the current mark.

Concept 2 · Club Crest

Alumni Sans, arc text in bold, monogram in black weight.

A roundel: program name in the ring, the stack in the center field. This is the most traditional read, and the strongest on apparel. A crest on a cap front or a tee chest reads instantly as a real club with a history, even a short one.

It is too detailed for a 16px favicon, so the site would pair it with Concept 1's bare stack for small uses. One geometry family, two presentations, same as the current nav-versus-favicon split.

Concept 3 · Trail Blaze

Big Shoulders ExtraBold inside a rounded keyline square.

The stack set in a tall condensed face inside a keyline frame, the way a trail blaze or route marker is signed. The two C's take the accent color, making the shared rail the designed moment. Drop to one color and it still works.

The frame gives it presence on fabric without filling a solid block, and the condensed letterforms keep it narrow enough for a beanie fold or a sleeve hit.

03 · Four color directions

Each direction shows its swatches, the site header and a page mocked in the palette, and a cap in the team colorway. Logos in the mocks use Concept 1; any concept takes any palette. All values are oklch, ready to drop into the token system.

A · Spruce & Birch

deep spruce green, birch-leaf gold, bark cream

The hillside above East Anchorage. Deep spruce as the identity color, birch gold as the warm accent, cream standing in for bark and snow. Tuned darker and warmer than UAA's kelly-and-gold so the two never read as the same kit: spruce is a forest color, UAA's is a court color.

B · Fireweed & Spruce

fireweed magenta on deep spruce

Fireweed lines every trail this program runs on from July to August. It is the most Alaskan color there is that nobody in town is wearing: clearly not Winter Stars red, clearly not East crimson, and unmistakable at 200 meters in a race pack. Spruce does the grounding so the magenta stays a flash, not a flood.

04 · Recommendation

Logo: Concept 1, Varsity Stack in Alumni Sans Black, with Concept 2's crest cut from the same letterforms reserved for apparel and patches. The stack carries the site, favicon, and cap front; the crest is the tee-chest and hoodie piece. One family, two uses.

Color: B, Fireweed & Spruce. It is the only direction no program in the state can be confused with, it photographs well against snow and against summer trails, and the story is already yours: the flower that grows where this team trains. Spruce & Birch is the strong second if you want something quieter.
